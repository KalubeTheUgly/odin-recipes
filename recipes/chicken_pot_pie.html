Chicken Pot Pie

Description

Chicken pot pie is a savory comfort food dish that combines tender chunks of chicken, vegetables (often carrots, peas, potatoes, and celery), and a creamy, seasoned gravy, all baked inside a flaky pie crust.

The crust can be a traditional double crust (top and bottom pastry) or a single top crust, sometimes puff pastry or biscuit dough. When baked, the crust turns golden and crisp, while the inside stays warm, rich, and hearty. The flavors are mild yet satisfying, with the creamy filling balancing the buttery crunch of the crust — making it a classic cold-weather favorite.

Ingredients

  • 2 cups cooked chicken, diced or shredded
  • 1 cup carrots, diced
  • 1 cup frozen peas
  • 1 cup potatoes, diced (optional but classic)
  • 1/2 cup celery, diced
  • 1/2 cup unsalted butter
  • 1/2 cup all-purpose flour
  • 2 cups chicken broth
  • 1 cup milk or half-and-half
  • 1 tsp salt (adjust to taste)
  • 1/2 tsp black pepper
  • 1/2 tsp garlic powder (optional)
  • 1/2 tsp dried thyme or poultry seasoning
  • 2 unbaked pie crusts (homemade or store-bought) (or use puff pastry for a flakier top)
  • 1 egg (for egg wash, optional)

Steps

  1. Preheat the oven to 425°F (220°C)
  2. Cook the veggies:
    • In a medium pot, boil carrots, potatoes, and celery for about 8–10 minutes until slightly tender. Drain and set aside.
  3. Make the creamy filling:
    • In a large skillet or saucepan, melt butter over medium heat.
    • Stir in flour and cook for 1–2 minutes to make a roux (thick paste).
    • Slowly whisk in chicken broth, then milk, stirring constantly until thick and smooth.
    • Season with salt, pepper, thyme, and garlic powder.
    • Stir in chicken, cooked vegetables, and peas. Remove from heat.
  4. Assemble the pie:
    • Place one pie crust into a 9-inch pie pan.
    • Pour the chicken mixture into the crust.
    • Cover with the second crust, sealing the edges and cutting small slits on top for steam to escape
    • Brush the top with a beaten egg for a golden finish (optional)
  5. Bake:
    • Place on a baking sheet (to catch drips) and bake for 30–35 minutes, or until crust is golden brown.
    • Let cool for 10 minutes before serving so the filling sets.
